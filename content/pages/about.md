## How I work

I like problems that start fuzzy and become clearer through shipping. The job is not to talk about AI in the abstract. The job is to decide where it creates leverage, define the human loop around it, and build something a team can actually run.

## Why people bring me in

- They have an idea but not a working system yet.
- They need someone who can move between business context and technical execution.
- They want faster decision loops without lowering the quality bar.

## What changed in my workflow because of AI

AI shortened the distance between concept and proof. It did not remove the need for judgment. The leverage comes from tighter loops, clearer systems, and better use of human attention.

## The kinds of problems I like

- Prototypes that help teams decide faster
- Internal tools that remove friction from repeated work
- AI workflows with explicit owners, checks, and outcomes
- Product systems that connect decision-making to execution

## How I collaborate

I prefer direct communication, crisp scopes, and visible progress. The best work usually comes from short loops, shared context, and willingness to pressure test assumptions early.
